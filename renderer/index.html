How should it look?

Lock your journal?

This is optional. You can skip it now and turn it on later in Settings.

Adding a PIN encrypts your notes on this computer, so the files cannot be read on their own. How well that holds up depends on the PIN you choose, and Flint will show you how long each one would take to guess.

Choose a PIN

At least 4 characters. Flint asks for this each time it opens.

Save your recovery code

This is the only way in if you forget your PIN. It is shown once and never stored, so keep it somewhere safe and separate from this computer.

Settings

Theme

Pick whatever's easiest on your eyes. System follows your Windows light or dark setting.

Accent colour

A little colour for buttons, links and highlights. It works with any theme.

Daily reminder

An optional nudge from this computer to write. It never leaves your machine, and it is skipped if you have already written that day.

Your streak

Pick any days you do not plan to write. A day off is stepped over, so it never breaks your streak.

Guided prompts

Optional structured boxes you can turn on for a day (Food, Sleep, People…). They are off by default, because Flint is free-form writing first.

Edit the guided prompts

Rename, add, reorder or remove them. Removing one only hides it. Anything you already wrote under it stays saved and still exports.

Entry templates

Reusable scaffolds you can drop into a day with the Template button under your writing.

Your activities

These are the choices in the "What did today hold?" picker under your writing. Put one on each line, and you can start a line with an emoji if you like.

Take your journal with you

Every day, oldest first. Text and PDF to read, Markdown to reuse anywhere, JSON to bring back later.

Importing a JSON file only adds days you do not already have. Nothing already written is overwritten.

Daily activities summary

A plain, print-ready record of your days and how they varied, built around everyday activities. Handy to keep for yourself, or to show someone who's helping you.

Backups to another folder

Flint can drop a dated copy of your journal into a folder you choose (a USB stick, or a folder you already sync), once a day. If your journal is encrypted, the copy is encrypted too.

Your patterns

Privacy and what Flint shares

  • Your notes never leave this computer. They're stored in plain files on your PC, and the window you write in cannot make any internet connection at all.
  • No account, no sync, no analytics, no tracking. Flint doesn't know who you are and never phones home about what you write.
  • Two things can go online, and only when you start them. The first is the optional update check (in Settings, under Updates). It only downloads a new version, and can never send your entries. Like any download it reveals your computer's IP to GitHub, and nothing else. The second is feedback you choose to send (the Feedback link at the foot of the window), which goes to a form service so it can reach the app's maker, and never includes any journal content. Leave both alone and Flint stays fully offline.
  • Exports stay with you. Saving a text file or PDF writes to your own Documents folder; copying puts it on your clipboard. Nothing is uploaded.
  • On disk: your entries are stored as readable files. Turn on encryption in Settings (Lock & encryption) to scramble them with a PIN, so they cannot be read without it.
